Rotunda de Carlos da Maia

A.k.aThe Three Lamps
LocatedSt. Anthony’s Parish

the Rotunda de Carlos da Maia is of typical Western architectural design with its central area a popular place for public activities and leisure. The periphery of the Rotunda is a network of 5 streets radiated outwards in all directions, namely Rua de Fernao Mendes Pinto, Rua da Restauração and Rua de Manuel de Arriaga.

Rua de Fernao Mendes Pinto

TrendNorth West - South East
The southeastRua de Francisco Xavier Pereira
The northwestEstrada de Coelho do Amaral

The Rua de Fernao Mendes Pinto and its tightly clustered streets nearby, like Rua da Emenda, Rua do Lu Cao and Rua do Rebanho, are a popular shopping area for the locals where shops and street vendors stand along the way with all kinds of goods to entertain the crowds of shoppers.

Rua de Manuel de Arriaga

TrendNortheast - Southwest
The northeastAvenida de Horta e Costa
The southwestRua de Afonso de Albuquerque

The Rua de Manuel de Arriaga gives direct access to Avenida de Horta e Costa, a main traffic artery, the Praça do Tap Seac and to the Mount Fortress Corridor just some distance up the street.

Rua da Restauração

Next toRua de Fernao Mendes Pinto /
Rua de Manuel de Arriaga
Leads toRua da Barca

The Rua da Restauração leads to Rua da Barca, another favorite shopping centre well-known for shops and earteries offering special delicacies and sales of fashionable products catered to the taste of youngsters.

The Rua da Barca opens the way to the San Kio district, an old area of Macao populated by temples and cultural attractions, like Lin Kai Temple, Seak Kam Dong Shrine and Pao Kong Temple.
