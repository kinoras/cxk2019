Southeast Asian Culture

There are many returned overseas Chinese in Myanmar, Thailand, Indonesia and other Southeast Asian countries. The Rotunda de Carlos da Maia are also the gathering place for Southeast Asian cuisine. During the "Southeast Asian Food Carnival" event, every night in the Rotunda de Carlos da Maia, there are Southeast Asian ethnic songs and dances. In addition, a number of Southeast Asian food stalls will be opened in the Guangfu Street to Rotunda de Carlos da Maia section. In addition to the food carnival, the Rotunda de Carlos da Maia is a gathering place for Burmese Chinese, and will also host the “Water Splash Festival”. In addition to the Splashing Water Carnival, the Rotunda de Carlos da Maia District will also hold a traditional food exhibition in Southeast Asia and Myanmar, called “Southeast Asian Food and Culture Street”.

Activities

The Rotunda de Carlos da Maia is a gathering place for overseas Chinese in Myanmar. It is also a “Water Splashing Festival” organized by MASSM, IAM and the Tourism Bureau since April 1995. The Songkran Festival is popular in Thailand, Myanmar, Cambodia, and Yunnan of China. It is equivalent to the New Year's holiday. People celebrate this year with clear water and pray for the disappointment of the past year. In addition to large-scale float parades and water-filling carnivals in various locations, the organizers will also hold traditional food exhibitions in Southeast Asia and Myanmar in the Sancha Light District, known as the “Southeast Asian Food and Culture Street” as a major project in the Songkran Festival.
In addition to the above activities, the Rotunda de Carlos da Maia will also hold a festive carnival such as the Bathing Buddha Festival, Happy Macau Street and New Year's Eve Countdown.
